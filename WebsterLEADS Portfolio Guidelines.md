WebsterLEADS - Portfolio Review Guidelines

What is Portfolio Review?
Portfolio Review is a summarization of your experiences during the four semester WebsterLEADS program and your leadership journey while in college. 

What is the purpose of Portfolio Review?
•	To ensure students admitted to the WebsterLEADS Program have demonstrated their leadership potential in mandatory classes and on campus leadership experiences. 
•	To provide an in-depth, constructive appraisal of your leadership at Webster University. 
•	To provide a process through which the WebsterLEADS Program can ensure a high level of leadership from its graduates. 

Who must complete a Portfolio Review?
A student pursuing a Leadership Certificate must pass Portfolio Review as a requirement of the program. If you fail your portfolio, you can submit it to your LEADS mentor again at an agreed upon time. If you fail a second time, you will not receive your LEADS certificate. 

When should I be prepared for Portfolio Review?
You are eligible after completion of the following  WebsterLEADS program requirements over three semesters. You will work with your mentor to turn in your portfolio by the end of the fourth semester.
1.	The Challenge of Leadership (EDUC 2750) - SEMESTER ONE
2.	Ethics and Leadership on Campus (ETHC 1000) - SEMESTER TWO
3.	Practical Leadership Experience 1 - SEMESTER TWO
4.	Practical Leadership Experience 2 - SEMESTER THREE

What do I include in my portfolio?
•	Personal leadership philosophy statement/Personal Mission Statement as developed in EDEX 2750 and a final version for portfolio. This will include a discussion of how the WebsterLEADS program has made you grow as a leader and an evaluation of your leadership strengths and weaknesses as they stand now compared to the beginning of the program.
•	ETHC 1000 Individual Reflection Paper
•	Summaries and reflections of your Practical Leadership experiences. 
•	Include other experiences that are applicable (ie. Study abroad, internships, etc. That were not your practical leadership experiences.)

What format is the portfolio submitted in?
Your portfolio should be submitted in a format that works best for you as a supplement to your resume or other existing academic portfolio documents.  This could be submitted as a resume addendum, video, or website.  Work with your mentor to determine what is the best fit for your portfolio. 

International Distinction
To receive international distinction, you must have completed at least one additional leadership experience that occurred while studying abroad or conducted during an international experience in St. Louis. This must be in addition to one of your Practical Leadership Experiences.
