Describe the leadership position you held:



As the Graphic Designer and Social Media Manager for Campus Activities, I was responsible for creating digital and print marketing materials to promote campus events. I also captured photo and video content during events for social media. Occasionally, I assisted event coordinators by serving as a versatile helping hand to ensure operations ran smoothly.





\---------------------------



Who did you serve in this role?



I served the Campus Activities event coordinators and event attendees, who were primarily fellow students.



\---------------------------



Reflect on the goals created for this position in your PLE proposal.  Did you reach those goals?  Why or Why not?



One of my primary goals was to experience coordinating an event from scratch. I achieved this twice: first during Welcome Week, where I hosted a large-scale event, and again in the spring semester during a Game Night collaboration between the Computer Science Club and the Video Game Fighting Club. I enjoyed the challenge of overseeing logistics, planning ahead, and ensuring everything was in place during the execution phase.



I also aimed to improve our sustainability practices. To reduce paper waste from individual event flyers, I implemented a monthly schedule printout that consolidated all events onto one page. Although this was eventually rolled back due to a dip in student involvement that required more aggressive individual marketing, the experience allowed me to reflect on my impact as a designer on environmental initiatives.



\---------------------------



What was the highlight of your experience?



A highlight of my experience was mentoring a newer colleague who felt overwhelmed by a social situation at work. I was able to step into a leadership role by guiding them on how to communicate professionally with superiors. By comforting them and explaining that a simple, honest conversation is often more effective than overthinking, I helped them navigate the situation with confidence.



\---------------------------



What major events or activities will you be involved with as part of this position?



I contributed to several major campus traditions, including:



* Headliner events
* Casino Night
* The Formal Dance
* Quad Fest
* Welcome Week
* Stress Relief Week



\---------------------------



What was the biggest challenge you faced in this role?  How did you address the issue or overcome the challenge?



The biggest challenge was navigating the backlash regarding the use of an AI-generated element in the designs for Casino Night. It was difficult because the online criticism felt personal. I addressed the issue by maintaining clear communication with my supervisor, Jennifer Stewart. I eventually had a very productive, respectful conversation with the individual who initiated the critique. We were able to explain our perspectives and acknowledge our disagreements professionally, which was far more fruitful than the social media outrage.



\---------------------------



For what reason did you select this as a Practical Leadership Experience?



Having worked for Campus Activities since my first few days at Webster, I have experienced incredible growth through my interactions with students and coworkers. I chose this role for my PLE because it provided the perfect environment to apply the leadership skills I’ve learned through the certification program. It offered a balance of familiarity based on my previous semesters of experience, and room for continued professional growth.



\---------------------------



How does this position benefit Webster University?



This position benefits the university by streamlining the design and social media workflow. By centralizing these tasks, I relieved the Graduate Assistants and event coordinators of the marketing burden, allowing them to focus entirely on the logistical success of their events.



\---------------------------



How will this position benefit your academic, professional or personal goals?



Professionally, this role helped me keep my design skills sharp and consistently polished. It allowed me to solve complex creative problems and ensured that I remained engaged in the creative process throughout the academic year.



\---------------------------



Is there anything else we need to know?



I entered this role with a commitment to being Campus Activities' best hiring decision. While I am naturally very task-oriented, this experience reminded me of the importance of being people-oriented, a core concept of our leadership theory classes. I deeply value the healthy, positive professional relationships I have built throughout this journey and am grateful for the entire experience.
