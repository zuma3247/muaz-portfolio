Describe the leadership position you held:



As President of the Computer Science Club, I was responsible for developing engaging event ideas that benefited students both academically and professionally. I coordinated with a committee of eight members and two advisors to ensure all initiatives were executed effectively.



\---------------------------



Who did you serve in this role?



I served the Computer Science Club members and supported the broader interests of the Walker School.



\---------------------------



Reflect on the goals created for this position in your PLE proposal.  Did you reach those goals?  Why or Why not?



In the past, the CS Club had a very serious tone, focusing primarily on professional or educational events. To create a better balance, I introduced "fun" events, such as game nights.



I also aimed to raise awareness about Artificial Intelligence on campus. I achieved this through the AI Crossroads panel, where I invited experts to discuss the various advantages and disadvantages of AI. Finally, I sought to recruit passionate members to drive the club forward. While we held successful elections last month, many of the positions were filled by current committee members stepping up rather than "fresh faces." Student involvement across campus seemed to decline this academic year, which likely contributed to the challenge of finding as many new passionate members as I had hoped for.



\---------------------------



What was the highlight of your experience?



Webster Hackfest '26 was the definitive highlight. It was an interdisciplinary hackathon held in collaboration with the Business Club and the Game Design Guild. Coordinating an entire weekend of competition involved many moving parts and unexpected challenges, such as last-minute judge cancellations and double-booked spaces. Managing 29 organizers and volunteers to keep the event running smoothly was a significant win for my leadership development. 



\---------------------------



What major events or activities will you be involved with as part of this position?



I was involved in all of the activities of the club. That included:

\- Welcome meetings and study session collaborations with the Academic Resource Center. 

* Hackathon \& capture the flag (CTF) workshops 
* Byte Bash, which is a beginner-friendly coding competition. 
* Field trip to Worldwide Technology 
* The AI Crossroads panel 
* Among Us Game Night
* Game Night Collaboration with Campus Activities
* Hackfest '26 
* Agentic AI workshop with Christian Johnson





\---------------------------



What was the biggest challenge you faced in this role?  How did you address the issue or overcome the challenge?



My officers had very demanding schedules, making task delegation difficult. Initially, I considered being stricter to hold them accountable, but I eventually realized that they are responsible adults managing their own priorities. Once I accepted this, it became easier to move forward by working with whoever was available and distributing tasks accordingly.

For what reason did you select this as a Practical Leadership Experience?



I began my journey with WebsterLEADS while serving as Vice President of the Computer Science Club. As I progressed through the certification program, I began to understand my leadership style and looked for ways to apply what I had learned. Since I was elected President for the following year, this role was the ideal PLE, serving as a direct continuation of my leadership journey.





\---------------------------



How does this position benefit Webster University?



By positioning a student organization at the forefront of industry networking and professional competitions, we send a message that Webster University students are proactive. 



We aren't waiting for opportunities to come to us. 

We are actively creating them. 



Effective leadership within these organizations elevates the university's reputation in the professional community.



\---------------------------



How will this position benefit your academic, professional or personal goals?



Leadership is one of the most vital soft skills in the professional world. This role allowed me to discover who I am as a leader by applying WebsterLEADS theory to practical, real-world scenarios. It has bridged the gap between academic knowledge and professional execution.



\---------------------------



Is there anything else we need to know?



Despite the challenges, I am grateful I remained humble enough to seek guidance such as reaching out to Jen whenever I needed help navigating a hurdle. I also identified areas for personal growth, specifically in learning how to delegate without feeling like I am burdening my team and refining my communication to ensure my instructions are always crystal clear.
